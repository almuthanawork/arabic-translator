# Humanizer Checklist for Translation Output

After completing the literary translation, scan your English output for these AI writing patterns. This checklist is derived from Wikipedia's "Signs of AI writing" guide and the blader/humanizer skill.

## Quick Scan Categories

### Content Patterns
- [ ] **Significance inflation**: "stands as", "is a testament", "pivotal moment", "vital role", "underscores its importance", "setting the stage for", "indelible mark"
- [ ] **Notability name-dropping**: listing media outlets or awards without context
- [ ] **Superficial -ing analyses**: "highlighting...", "underscoring...", "emphasizing...", "reflecting...", "symbolizing...", "showcasing..."
- [ ] **Promotional language**: "boasts", "vibrant", "profound", "groundbreaking", "renowned", "breathtaking", "stunning", "nestled"
- [ ] **Vague attributions**: "experts argue", "observers note", "some critics argue", "industry reports"
- [ ] **Formulaic challenges sections**: "Despite its... faces challenges... Despite these challenges..."

### Language Patterns
- [ ] **AI vocabulary**: Additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key (adj), landscape (abstract), pivotal, showcase, tapestry (abstract), testament, underscore (verb), valuable, vibrant, multifaceted, nuanced
- [ ] **Copula avoidance**: "serves as", "stands as", "functions as" instead of simple "is"/"are"
- [ ] **Negative parallelisms**: "Not only...but...", "It's not just about..., it's..."
- [ ] **Rule of three**: forcing ideas into groups of three for false comprehensiveness
- [ ] **Synonym cycling**: swapping "protagonist"/"main character"/"central figure"/"hero" to avoid repetition
- [ ] **False ranges**: "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't on a meaningful scale

### Style Patterns
- [ ] **Em dash overuse**: more than one em dash per paragraph is suspicious
- [ ] **Boldface overuse**: mechanical emphasis on phrases
- [ ] **Inline-header lists**: "**Category:** description" bullet points
- [ ] **Title Case headings**: capitalizing all main words
- [ ] **Curly quotes**: use straight quotes ("...") not curly ("...")

### Communication Patterns
- [ ] **Chatbot artifacts**: "I hope this helps", "Let me know", "Here is a..."
- [ ] **Knowledge-cutoff disclaimers**: "as of", "based on available information"
- [ ] **Sycophantic tone**: "Great question!", "Absolutely!", "You're right"

### Filler and Hedging
- [ ] **Filler phrases**: "In order to", "Due to the fact that", "It is important to note that"
- [ ] **Excessive hedging**: "could potentially possibly be argued that might"
- [ ] **Generic positive conclusions**: "The future looks bright", "exciting times lie ahead"
- [ ] **Hyphenated word pair overuse**: perfectly consistent hyphenation of common compounds

## The Two-Pass System

**Pass 1**: Fix everything you found in the scan above. Rewrite affected sections while preserving the translation's fidelity to the Arabic source.

**Pass 2 (Self-Audit)**: Read your output fresh and ask: "What makes this obviously AI generated?" List the remaining tells (if any), then fix them.

## Translation-Specific AI Patterns

These are patterns that specifically emerge in AI-generated translations:

- **Over-smooth transitions**: every paragraph connects too neatly to the next
- **Emotional flattening**: everything reduced to the same neutral register
- **Explanatory insertions**: adding context the original doesn't provide, making the translation feel like a textbook
- **Consistent sentence length**: natural translated prose varies wildly in sentence length
- **Loss of the author's quirks**: the author's unusual phrasing or unconventional choices get "corrected" into standard English
- **Symmetry addiction**: making every structure balanced when the original is deliberately asymmetric

## What NOT to do

Don't let the humanizer pass damage the translation quality. The literary translation takes priority. If removing an "AI pattern" would weaken the fidelity to the Arabic source, keep the translation as-is. The humanizer is a polish step, not a rewrite step.
